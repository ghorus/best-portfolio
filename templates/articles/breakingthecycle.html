Breaking the Cycle: Overcoming Burnout, Boosting Brain Health, and Finding Focus in High-Stress Times

As a software engineer and entrepreneur, the past few months have been some of the most challenging times of my life. After being laid off and sending out over 400 applications without landing a job offer, I've faced chronic stress, burnout, and discouragement. Balancing the need to make money, pursue personal growth, and sustain my energy has left me feeling lost and overwhelmed. This article explores practical strategies to break free from these struggles, optimize brain health, and regain focus. Whether you're navigating financial pressure, job searches, or building your own business, this guide shares insights and tools that have worked for me to prioritize well-being and performance.

1. Mindset Reset: Reframing Overthinking and Anxiety

Overthinking and perfectionism often lead to paralysis. I’ve found myself overanalyzing everything—wanting to be super perfect for entrepreneurship while also feeling discouraged by doing so much with no results. One strategy that has helped me calm my body and mind is the 4-7-8 breathing exercise, which involves inhaling for 4 seconds, holding for 7 seconds, and exhaling for 8 seconds. This calms the nervous system and reduces anxiety. From there, I focus on positive thinking by taking small steps daily and writing down what I’ve tried—what worked, what didn’t, and why. Journaling about my goals and values has also helped me stay focused and more intentional with my efforts.

2. Brain and Body Optimization: Nutrition

Chronic low energy has been one of my biggest struggles. I’ve often wondered what nutrients are even worth considering. I’ve started small by focusing more on eating vegetables and fruits, especially when my wife encourages me to stick to healthier choices even when I don’t feel like it. I also make an effort to avoid junk foods, like blueberry muffins that I crave but ultimately make me feel icky afterward.

3. Prioritization and Focus: Balancing Competing Demands

I constantly feel torn between focusing on making money, personal growth, and school. Prioritizing tasks is key:

  • Focus on tasks that directly impact income first, like possibly applying to work for 2 jobs part-time.
  • Dedicate fixed time slots for personal growth or skill-building to avoid neglecting my spiritual needs.
  • Break large goals into manageable milestones to stay motivated.

4. Preventing Burnout: Sustainable Productivity Habits

Burnout has been a recurring theme in my life, especially after working repetitive jobs like being a cashier, warehouse associate, and dishwasher. Here are ways I've tried preventing it:

  • Take micro-breaks during work hours to recharge.
  • Practice mindfulness daily. I think about what makes work meaningful and what new things I can learn from my job. It helps me try and make the best out of a repetitive, mundane job.
  • Avoid any potential drama at work.
  • Voice any concerns and questions about work, such as career development, resources about 401k, or performance feedback.
  • Engage in hobbies or creative outlets to maintain balance and joy. As someone who doesn't have a big social network, my outlet is at the library. And especially because I'm an overthinker, reading books has helped with that energy.
  • Diversify your work. I think this also applies to jobs. You could work 2 part-time jobs instead of 1 full-time job.
